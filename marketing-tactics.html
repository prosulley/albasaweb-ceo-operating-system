Core principle
In Ghana's B2B market — especially law firms and real estate — nobody buys software from a stranger's ad. They buy from someone they have seen at an event, read an article by, or been referred to by a colleague they trust. Every marketing tactic you run must build that trust trail before it asks for a sale.

The one metric that tells you if your marketing is working

Discovery calls booked per week — not followers, not likes

The only number that matters is how many new people sit across from you each week to hear what ALBASA WEB does. Target: 2 discovery calls/week by Month 3. Every tactic you run should be traceable back to whether it produced this outcome.

1. Google Business Profile — free, high-impact, do this today
1

Claim and complete your profile at maps.google.com/business

Fill every field: services (web development, cybersecurity, penetration testing), service areas (Accra, Tema, Kumasi), business description using Ghana-specific language — "MoMo-integrated websites," "DPC-compliant," "3G-optimised." Add photos of your team and work. This is how law firms and real estate offices search "web developer Accra" — if you're not here, you don't exist to them.

Target: 5-star average from 10+ Google reviews within 90 days. Ask every satisfied client directly, by name.
2

Local SEO — rank for queries your clients actually type

Your albasaweb.com needs a location page targeting "web development company in Accra, Ghana," individual service pages, and blog posts answering what your clients search for. One keyword-optimised post per week dominates your niche within 6 months — especially in Ghana where local digital content competition is thin.

Priority keywords: "web developer Accra Ghana" — "cybersecurity company Ghana" — "e-commerce website Ghana small business" — "penetration testing Accra"
3

WhatsApp Business — your Ghana-native CRM, not just a chat app

Set up a Business account with: a service catalogue showing your 3 tiers, automated greeting and away messages, and quick-reply templates for "What's your pricing?" Create a broadcast list of warm leads — send a monthly value message (not a sales pitch) to keep ALBASA top of mind. Every contact you have spoken to goes in this list.

Monthly broadcast idea: "3 things Accra businesses got wrong with their websites this month — and how to fix them." Short. Useful. Never pushy.
2. LinkedIn — your primary B2B sales channel
1

Profile optimisation — both founders must look like authorities, not job seekers

Suleman's headline: "CEO, ALBASA WEB | Web Development & Cybersecurity for Ghanaian Businesses | PMP® | PMI-ACP®". Mubarak's headline: "CTO, ALBASA WEB | Ethical Hacker | AI-Powered Penetration Testing | Cybersecurity for Ghana SMEs". Featured section links to albasaweb.com and one case study. Both profiles must mention Ghana, Accra, and the specific sectors you serve.

2

Outbound connection campaign — 50 targeted connections per week, not random

Use LinkedIn search: Location = Ghana, Industry = Legal Services / Real Estate / Retail, Seniority = Owner / Managing Partner / CEO / Director. Send a connection request with a personalised note — never a sales pitch. Example: "Hi [Name], I saw your firm handles corporate law in Accra. I work with law firms on their digital infrastructure — would love to connect." Sell in conversation, not in the request.

Conversion expectation: 50 requests → 15 accepted → 3 reply → 1 discovery call. One new prospect per week from LinkedIn alone.
3

Posting — 3 posts per week, three types in rotation

Type A (Monday): insight — "Why Accra law firms are losing clients to firms with better websites." Type B (Wednesday): case study snippet — "We rebuilt a client's site. Speed score went from 34 to 91. Here's what changed." Type C (Friday): behind the scenes — "Building a MoMo checkout for a Tema e-commerce brand. Here's the admin dashboard." Alternate between Suleman and Mubarak as authors.

3. Lead magnet — turn strangers into warm prospects automatically
1

Build the "Ghana Business Website Health Checker" — a free audit tool

A 10-question form that scores a business across: website speed, mobile performance, payment integration, local SEO, security basics, and DPC compliance. At the end, it emails them a 2-page PDF report — personalised with their score — that positions each gap as something ALBASA WEB solves. Embed on your homepage. Promote everywhere. This runs 24/7 without you.

Build with: Typeform → Google Sheets → PDF report template → Gmail auto-send. Or build it as a Next.js page on albasaweb.com and generate the report server-side. One week to build. Generates leads forever.
2

Free "Digital Readiness Call" — the discovery call reframed

Add a Calendly link to your website and LinkedIn. Label it: "Free 30-min digital readiness review — we'll tell you exactly what's holding your business back online." This removes the word "sales." Business owners feel they're getting value. You get a qualified prospect on the phone. Book 8 per month. Close 2–3. That's your growth engine.

Ghana associations — your fastest path to warm, high-value leads
1

Ghana Bar Association (GBA) — your law firm entry channel

Attend quarterly dinners and continuing legal education (CLE) events. Sponsor a refreshment table (GHC 1,500–3,000) and put your brochure on every seat. Offer to run a free 45-minute CLE session: "Cybersecurity and Data Compliance for Law Firms in Ghana." This qualifies as CLE credit for lawyers and positions you as the expert they need.

Action: Find GBA Accra Regional on LinkedIn. Message the secretary asking about upcoming events and sponsorship slots. Do this this week.
2

Ghana Real Estate Developers Association (GREDA) + Ghana Association of Realtors (GAR)

These two associations run monthly networking events and an annual conference. Join as a technology partner — not a member. Offer to speak at their next event: "How Ghanaian real estate firms are losing diaspora buyers — and how to fix it in 6 weeks." Pain-first talk, not a sales pitch. You will walk out with 10–15 business cards from developers who want to follow up.

Key event: GREDA Annual Summit (usually Q3/Q4). Start the conversation now — speaking slots fill months in advance.
3

Ghana Chamber of Commerce + Accra Business Club

Register ALBASA WEB as a member. Attend monthly breakfasts. The rule at these events: listen more than you talk. Ask every person you meet, "What's the biggest challenge your business is facing digitally?" The answers will shape your next proposal template and write your next LinkedIn post.

4

Ghana Tech Summit + Impact Hub Accra + MEST Africa

Your credibility-building events — where you meet peers, potential partners, and people who refer enterprise clients. Exhibit if budget allows (GHC 2,000–5,000 for a small booth). Every senior developer or tech founder you meet here is a potential referral partner for clients they can't serve.

University partnerships — your talent pipeline doubles as a marketing channel
1

Guest lectures at KNUST, UG, Ashesi, and Regent — once per semester

Offer a 1-hour lecture to final-year CS or business students on "Building a software company in Ghana." You get: brand exposure to 200+ future professionals and business owners, direct access to intern candidates, and credibility as an industry practitioner. Many students in that room become decision-makers at your target companies within 5 years. Your IT teaching background makes this effortless.

Each lecture generates 1–2 intern applications and 3–5 LinkedIn connections who follow your company page. Over time this builds a warm network of hundreds.
The ALBASA content engine — publish to be found, not to be famous

Every piece of content serves one of three jobs: attract a stranger, convert a prospect, or retain a client. Know which job each piece is doing before you write it.

1

The ALBASA blog — 2 posts per month, SEO-first, Ghana-specific

Every post targets one search query your ideal client types into Google. Write to answer that question completely — then mention ALBASA WEB naturally at the end. No fluff. No generic "Top 10 Web Design Tips." Ghana-specific, sector-specific, actionable.

Case studies — your most powerful sales content
1

One case study per completed project — published on albasaweb.com and LinkedIn

Format: (1) Client situation — what was broken. (2) What we built — 2 sentences, no jargon. (3) Measurable result — speed score, conversion rate, security vulnerabilities patched, time saved. (4) Client quote. 300 words maximum. One honest case study with real numbers closes more deals than ten polished blog posts.

If a client won't allow their name: "A mid-sized law firm in Osu, Accra." The story still works — the result is what converts.
2

The 30-second Loom video — your secret weapon for e-commerce cold outreach

Find an Instagram business in Accra with 3,000+ followers and no proper website. Record 30 seconds: show their Instagram page, show a mock of their storefront, show the MoMo checkout. Send via DM: "I built this mock for your brand in 20 minutes — imagine what the real thing would do for your Sunday sales." Personal. Visual. Specific. Converts at 15–20% to a reply — nothing else comes close for e-commerce cold outreach.

Referral — the most powerful and most neglected channel in Ghana B2B

In Ghana, most B2B deals happen because "someone told me about you." Build the system that makes referrals deliberate, not accidental.

1

The referral partner network — 10 strategic partners, not 100 random contacts

Identify people whose clients overlap with yours but whose services don't compete: accountants and auditors (their clients need digital invoicing and dashboards), chartered surveyors (their clients are real estate developers), corporate lawyers (their clients need data compliance portals), business consultants (their SME clients need full digital infrastructure), and insurance brokers (whose clients are exactly the law firms and real estate firms you target).

Approach: "I refer clients your way when they need accounting help. In return, when your clients ask about their website or cybersecurity, I'd love to be who you recommend." Formalise with a 10–15% referral fee on the first contract once it becomes consistent.
2

Past client activation — the easiest revenue you will ever generate

Every client you've delivered for is a referral asset. Three months after project close, send a personal message: "Hope the site is performing well — any feedback? If you know any other [law firm / real estate office / retailer] who needs what we built for you, I'd love an introduction." A warm referral from a satisfied client closes at 60–70%. A cold LinkedIn connection closes at 3–5%. Do the maths.

Build a referral follow-up calendar in HubSpot: every closed project triggers a reminder at 90 days, 6 months, and 12 months to touch base personally.
3

The ALBASA formal referral programme — clear structure, clear incentive

Create a one-page referral programme document. Anyone who refers a client that signs a contract worth GHC 5,000+ receives their choice of: 10% cash commission on the first project invoice, a free cybersecurity assessment for their own business (worth GHC 2,000), or a premium restaurant gift card. Document it. Share it with every partner and past client. Make referring you feel rewarding, not transactional.

4

Suleman's Ghana Gas network — an untapped professional asset

Your Ghana Gas colleagues, Ghana Gas Senior Association contacts, and industry peers know dozens of business owners who need exactly what you build. A personal message to 50 professional contacts — "I've started a digital company. If you know anyone who needs a website or cybersecurity audit, I'd love an introduction" — will generate 5–10 conversations within a week. Do not underestimate the power of your existing credibility in a professional network that already trusts you.

Law firms — trust-based, event-led, compliance-anchored

Lead with the Data Protection Act 2012 — not with "great websites"

Law firms care about liability above all else. Your opening line is never "we build great websites." It is: "Ghana's Data Protection Act requires firms handling client data to implement appropriate security measures. Most law firms in Accra are non-compliant today. We do a 3-hour audit that shows exactly where your risks are." A managing partner will always agree to that conversation.

Offer a free DPC compliance audit as the entry product

One firm. Three hours. Free. At the end, produce a 2-page report listing their 5 biggest digital risks. Offer to fix them. This converts at 40–50% to a paid engagement. Do this for 3 firms. Collect testimonials. Then charge GHC 2,500 for the audit. The audit pays for itself by selling the fix.

Content alongside: "Data Protection Compliance Checklist for Ghanaian Law Firms" — a free PDF download that generates inbound leads passively.

Monthly email newsletter to law firm contacts — "The Digital Brief"

One-page monthly email: 1 Ghana tech regulation update, 1 cybersecurity threat law firms should know about, 1 tip for improving client digital experience. 300 words. Consistent. Free. Keeps you top of mind for the moment they are ready — typically quarterly budget cycles.

Real estate — diaspora-anchored, visual-first, conversion-focused

Lead with the diaspora payment problem — not with "modern websites"

Your opening line to any real estate developer: "How do your diaspora buyers in the UK or US pay a reservation fee on a property today?" The answer is always: "Bank transfer, then they WhatsApp us the receipt." Your response: "We can give them a payment link they click from London, pay in their currency, and you receive GHC in your Paystack account in 24 hours. No WhatsApp receipts. No lost leads." That conversation always ends with a meeting.

Build one speculative real estate website mock — use it in every pitch

Pick a real Accra real estate company with a poor website. Build a beautiful mock — property listing page, virtual tour placeholder, "Reserve this property" button showing the MoMo/card payment flow. Never show the client without permission. Show it in your discovery meeting. "Here is what we could build for a company like yours." Close rate with a visual mock is 3× higher than words alone.

E-commerce — volume play, Instagram-first, speed as the pitch

Target Instagram businesses directly with a 30-second Loom video

Search Instagram hashtags: #AccraBusiness, #GhanaFashion, #AccraFood, #MadeInGhana, #GhanaBeauty. Find accounts with 3,000–50,000 followers actively selling via DM. Record 30 seconds: show their Instagram page, show a mock of their storefront, show the MoMo checkout. Send via DM: "I built this mock for your brand in 20 minutes — imagine what the real thing would do for your Sunday sales."

Package Siri Stores as a fixed-price product — remove decision friction

Pricing by quotation kills e-commerce deals. Publish a fixed package on your website: "E-commerce Starter — GHC 8,000 — ready in 4 weeks. Includes: product catalogue, MoMo + card checkout, order management dashboard, WhatsApp OTP verification, mobile-optimised." A fixed price with a fixed timeline eliminates the "let me think about it" response.
